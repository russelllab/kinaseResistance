CC   -!- FUNCTION: AKT1 is one of 3 closely related serine/threonine-protein
CC       kinases (AKT1, AKT2 and AKT3) called the AKT kinase, and which regulate
CC       many processes including metabolism, proliferation, cell survival,
CC       growth and angiogenesis (PubMed:15526160, PubMed:11882383,
CC       PubMed:21620960, PubMed:21432781). This is mediated through serine
CC       and/or threonine phosphorylation of a range of downstream substrates
CC       (PubMed:15526160, PubMed:11882383, PubMed:21620960, PubMed:21432781).
CC       Over 100 substrate candidates have been reported so far, but for most
CC       of them, no isoform specificity has been reported (PubMed:15526160,
CC       PubMed:11882383, PubMed:21620960, PubMed:21432781). AKT is responsible
CC       of the regulation of glucose uptake by mediating insulin-induced
CC       translocation of the SLC2A4/GLUT4 glucose transporter to the cell
CC       surface (By similarity). Phosphorylation of PTPN1 at 'Ser-50'
CC       negatively modulates its phosphatase activity preventing
CC       dephosphorylation of the insulin receptor and the attenuation of
CC       insulin signaling (By similarity). Phosphorylation of TBC1D4 triggers
CC       the binding of this effector to inhibitory 14-3-3 proteins, which is
CC       required for insulin-stimulated glucose transport (PubMed:11994271).
CC       AKT regulates also the storage of glucose in the form of glycogen by
CC       phosphorylating GSK3A at 'Ser-21' and GSK3B at 'Ser-9', resulting in
CC       inhibition of its kinase activity (By similarity). Phosphorylation of
CC       GSK3 isoforms by AKT is also thought to be one mechanism by which cell
CC       proliferation is driven (By similarity). AKT regulates also cell
CC       survival via the phosphorylation of MAP3K5 (apoptosis signal-related
CC       kinase) (PubMed:11154276). Phosphorylation of 'Ser-83' decreases MAP3K5
CC       kinase activity stimulated by oxidative stress and thereby prevents
CC       apoptosis (PubMed:11154276). AKT mediates insulin-stimulated protein
CC       synthesis by phosphorylating TSC2 at 'Ser-939' and 'Thr-1462', thereby
CC       activating the TORC1 signaling pathway, and leading to both
CC       phosphorylation of 4E-BP1 and in activation of RPS6KB1
CC       (PubMed:12150915). Also regulates the TORC1 signaling pathway by
CC       catalyzing phosphorylation of CASTOR1 (PubMed:33594058). AKT is
CC       involved in the phosphorylation of members of the FOXO factors
CC       (Forkhead family of transcription factors), leading to binding of 14-3-
CC       3 proteins and cytoplasmic localization (PubMed:10358075). In
CC       particular, FOXO1 is phosphorylated at 'Thr-24', 'Ser-256' and 'Ser-
CC       319' (PubMed:10358075). FOXO3 and FOXO4 are phosphorylated on
CC       equivalent sites (PubMed:10358075). AKT has an important role in the
CC       regulation of NF-kappa-B-dependent gene transcription and positively
CC       regulates the activity of CREB1 (cyclic AMP (cAMP)-response element
CC       binding protein) (PubMed:9829964). The phosphorylation of CREB1 induces
CC       the binding of accessory proteins that are necessary for the
CC       transcription of pro-survival genes such as BCL2 and MCL1
CC       (PubMed:9829964). AKT phosphorylates 'Ser-454' on ATP citrate lyase
CC       (ACLY), thereby potentially regulating ACLY activity and fatty acid
CC       synthesis (By similarity). Activates the 3B isoform of cyclic
CC       nucleotide phosphodiesterase (PDE3B) via phosphorylation of 'Ser-273',
CC       resulting in reduced cyclic AMP levels and inhibition of lipolysis (By
CC       similarity). Phosphorylates PIKFYVE on 'Ser-318', which results in
CC       increased PI(3)P-5 activity (By similarity). The Rho GTPase-activating
CC       protein DLC1 is another substrate and its phosphorylation is implicated
CC       in the regulation cell proliferation and cell growth. AKT plays a role
CC       as key modulator of the AKT-mTOR signaling pathway controlling the
CC       tempo of the process of newborn neurons integration during adult
CC       neurogenesis, including correct neuron positioning, dendritic
CC       development and synapse formation (By similarity). Signals downstream
CC       of phosphatidylinositol 3-kinase (PI(3)K) to mediate the effects of
CC       various growth factors such as platelet-derived growth factor (PDGF),
CC       epidermal growth factor (EGF), insulin and insulin-like growth factor I
CC       (IGF-I) (PubMed:12176338, PubMed:12964941). AKT mediates the
CC       antiapoptotic effects of IGF-I (By similarity). Essential for the
CC       SPATA13-mediated regulation of cell migration and adhesion assembly and
CC       disassembly (PubMed:19934221). May be involved in the regulation of the
CC       placental development (By similarity). Phosphorylates STK4/MST1 at
CC       'Thr-120' and 'Thr-387' leading to inhibition of its: kinase activity,
CC       nuclear translocation, autophosphorylation and ability to phosphorylate
CC       FOXO3 (PubMed:17726016). Phosphorylates STK3/MST2 at 'Thr-117' and
CC       'Thr-384' leading to inhibition of its: cleavage, kinase activity,
CC       autophosphorylation at Thr-180, binding to RASSF1 and nuclear
CC       translocation (PubMed:20086174, PubMed:20231902). Phosphorylates SRPK2
CC       and enhances its kinase activity towards SRSF2 and ACIN1 and promotes
CC       its nuclear translocation (PubMed:19592491). Phosphorylates RAF1 at
CC       'Ser-259' and negatively regulates its activity (PubMed:10576742).
CC       Phosphorylation of BAD stimulates its pro-apoptotic activity
CC       (PubMed:10926925). Phosphorylates KAT6A at 'Thr-369' and this
CC       phosphorylation inhibits the interaction of KAT6A with PML and
CC       negatively regulates its acetylation activity towards p53/TP53
CC       (PubMed:23431171). Phosphorylates palladin (PALLD), modulating
CC       cytoskeletal organization and cell motility (PubMed:20471940).
CC       Phosphorylates prohibitin (PHB), playing an important role in cell
CC       metabolism and proliferation (PubMed:18507042). Phosphorylates CDKN1A,
CC       for which phosphorylation at 'Thr-145' induces its release from CDK2
CC       and cytoplasmic relocalization (PubMed:16982699). These recent findings
CC       indicate that the AKT1 isoform has a more specific role in cell
CC       motility and proliferation (PubMed:16139227). Phosphorylates CLK2
CC       thereby controlling cell survival to ionizing radiation
CC       (PubMed:20682768). Phosphorylates PCK1 at 'Ser-90', reducing the
CC       binding affinity of PCK1 to oxaloacetate and changing PCK1 into an
CC       atypical protein kinase activity using GTP as donor (PubMed:32322062).
CC       Also acts as an activator of TMEM175 potassium channel activity in
CC       response to growth factors: forms the lysoK(GF) complex together with
CC       TMEM175 and acts by promoting TMEM175 channel activation, independently
CC       of its protein kinase activity (PubMed:32228865).
